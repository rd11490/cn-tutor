## 📚 GRAMMAR

Today's grammar point: **愿不愿意 and other A-not-A question forms**

You already saw it in the passage: **愿不愿意一起去？**

You know how to ask yes/no questions with 吗. But there's another very natural way — the **A-not-A structure**:

> Verb + 不 + Verb = "Do you or don't you [verb]?"

It's more direct and colloquial than 吗 questions. Examples:

| A-not-A | 吗 version | Meaning |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| 你去不去？ | 你去吗？ | Are you going? |
| 你喜不喜欢？ | 你喜欢吗？ | Do you like it? |
| 你愿不愿意？ | 你愿意吗？ | Are you willing? |
| 你累不累？ | 你累吗？ | Are you tired? |

With **adjectives**, same pattern: 好不好、忙不忙、贵不贵
