## 📚 GRAMMAR — Formal register: 请注意 / 不得 / 应该 in announcements

In the passage you just read, you saw three ways of giving instructions formally:

| Formal | Meaning | Conversational equivalent |
|--------|---------|--------------------------|
| **请注意** | Please note / pay attention | 你要注意 |
| **不得** + verb | Must not / are not permitted to | 不可以, 不能 |
| **应** / **应该** | Should / ought to | 应该 (same, but 应 alone is more formal) |

The big one to focus on today is **不得** — you haven't seen this before.

**不得** (bù dé) means "must not / it is not permitted to." It's stronger and more formal than 不能 or 不可以. You'll see it in rules, notices, and official documents.

Structure: **不得 + verb (+ object)**

> 不得穿校服参加比赛。— You must not wear school uniform to compete.
> 不得迟到。— Lateness is not permitted.
> 不得使用手机。— Mobile phones must not be used.

Notice how 不得 sentences are often short and direct — no subject needed. That's very typical of formal written rules.
