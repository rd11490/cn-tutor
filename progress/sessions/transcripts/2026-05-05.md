## 📝 GRAMMAR — Tag Questions & Rhetorical Questions

Last session we covered A-not-A questions. Today we're going one step further — **tag questions and rhetorical questions**. These are what make Mandarin conversation feel *alive*.

---

### Part 1: Tag Questions

In English you say *"It's cold, isn't it?"* or *"You like coffee, right?"*

In Chinese, the most common tags are:

| Tag | Feeling |
|-----|---------|
| **对吧？** | "Right? / Isn't that so?" — seeking confirmation |
| **是吧？** | "Right? / I assume so?" — slightly softer, more uncertain |
| **你说呢？** | "What do you think? / Don't you agree?" — inviting opinion |
| **吧** (alone) | Softens a statement into a gentle assumption |

**Examples:**
> 你也觉得这里的咖啡很好喝，对吧？
> *(You also think the coffee here is good, right?)*

> 他没来，是吧？
> *(He didn't come, I assume?)*

> 这件事不容易，你说呢？
> *(This isn't easy, what do you think?)*

---

### Part 2: Rhetorical Questions

These *look* like questions but are actually strong statements. No answer is expected.

| Structure | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **不是...吗？** | "Isn't it...?" — emphasizes the obvious |
| **哪里...？** | "How could...? / Where is the...?" — polite denial |
| **难道...吗？** | "Don't tell me...? / Surely not...?" — disbelief |

**Examples:**
> 你不是说你不累吗？
> *(Didn't you say you weren't tired? — implying: so why are you complaining?)*

> 哪里哪里。
> *(Not at all! — classic humble response to a compliment)*

> 难道你不知道吗？
> *(Don't tell me you didn't know?)*

---

### 🔁 DRILLS

I'll give you a situation. You form the sentence using the tag or rhetorical structure indicated. Always attempt first!

**Drill 1:** Your friend said the movie starts at 7pm. You want to confirm. Use **对吧**.

**Drill 5:** You and a friend are talking about how expensive the restaurant is. Invite their opinion using **你说呢**.
