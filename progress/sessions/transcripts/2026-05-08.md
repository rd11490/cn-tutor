## GRAMMAR — 连...都/也

Quick recap in English before we drill:

**Structure:** 连 + [emphasized thing] + 都/也 + [verb/result]

**What it does:** Highlights that even the most minimal, basic, or unexpected thing is affected. The thing after 连 is always what's being emphasized.

**Key tips:**
- 都 and 也 are interchangeable here — 都 is slightly more common
- The "emphasized thing" can be a noun, a verb/action, or a time amount
- Remember the 觉/睡不着 discussion — if it sounds clunky, try using the noun form after 连

**Examples:**
- 他连你的名字都不知道。— He doesn't even know your name.
- 我连觉都睡不着。— I can't even sleep.
- 她连一分钟都等不了。— She can't even wait one minute.

Really good — you got 不是...吗 perfect and 连觉都 is the right structure! Just one issue:

**连觉都不睡好** — 睡好 is a result complement meaning "sleep well/properly." When you negate a result complement, the 不 goes **inside** the complement, between the verb and the result:

- 睡好 ✅
- 睡**不**好 ✅ (can't sleep well)
- 不睡好 ❌

So: 连觉都**睡不好**
