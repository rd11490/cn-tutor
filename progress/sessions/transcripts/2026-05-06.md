## 📝 GRAMMAR — 比 Comparisons

This is one of the most common structures in spoken Chinese, and it came up naturally in today's reading. Let's get it solid.

---

### The Basic Structure

**A + 比 + B + [adjective]**

> 地铁比公共汽车快。
> The subway is faster than the bus.

Simple rule: **no 很 after the adjective.** This is the most common mistake. 比 already implies a difference, so 很 contradicts it.

❌ 地铁比公共汽车**很**快
✅ 地铁比公共汽车**快**

---

### Adding Degree

You can specify *how much* more with these:

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 一点儿 | a little more | 她比我高一点儿 |
| 多了 | much more | 香山比我家远多了 |
| 得多 | much more | 今天比昨天冷得多 |

Note: the degree word goes **AFTER** the adjective.

✅ 香山比我家**远多了**
❌ 香山比我家**多了远**

---

### 比较 (bǐjiào)

比较 means "relatively / comparatively" and is used **without** a second subject:

> 早一点儿比较好。— It's relatively better to go early.
> 这家饭馆比较便宜。— This restaurant is relatively cheap.

---

### Negative Comparisons

To say A is NOT more than B, use **没有**:

**A + 没有 + B + [adjective]**

> 公共汽车没有地铁快。
> The bus isn't as fast as the subway.
